Dashboard resume

The demand fluctuates based on its sensitivity. In line with this, this dashboard compares the results of three cases, with sensitivity factors of 1, 1.2, and 1.5, respectively, to demonstrate how they vary in response to changes in the factor.

System results

As expected, the demand varies according to sensitivity factor value.

Observing that the capacity is exceeded in the case with a sensitivity factor of 1.5, it's understandable why a deficit occurs in this particular scenario.

Thermal results

As the demand increases, the following adjustments occur: first, the least costly thermal plant (T1) increases its dispatch up to its maximum capacity; second, the more expensive thermal plant (T2) increases its generation up to its maximum capacity as well.

Marginal cost results

In the first two cases (factors 1 and 1.2), there is no deficit, and as a result, the marginal cost is determined by the cost of the marginal plant. However, in the last case, as expected, the model detects a deficit, and therefore, the local marginal price is defined by the deficit cost.
